## What you will ride

Seven days crossing between Argentina and Chile through Patagonia and Carretera Austral. Closed forests, fjords, turquoise lakes, and quiet border passes. We cross Paso Futaleufú, ride along Lago General Carrera, and return through Paso Roballos, one of the most powerful roads in the south.

## The route

### Day 1 — Bariloche → Trevelin

30% gravel · 306 km

A mix of pavement and gravel with no physical demand. The ideal day to find rhythm. We leave Bariloche heading south on Ruta 40, one of Patagonia's classic sections. The detour at Cholila begins Parque Nacional Los Alerces, alternating pavement and gravel beside rivers, lakes, and closed forest.

**Highlights:** El Bolsón · Lago Puelo · Parque Nacional Los Alerces · Lago Rivadavia and Futalaufquen.

### Day 2 — Trevelin → Paso Futaleufú → Puerto Cisnes

20% gravel · 325 km

We visit the tulip fields, unique in the south. Then we continue toward the Futaleufú border pass, a short but beautiful section of landscape, color, and rivers. With Carretera Austral, the route turns wilder: gravel and constant curves. We cross closed forest, rivers, and mountain zones to Puyuhuapi and then Puerto Cisnes. The arrival marks the end of Argentina and the full entry into Carretera Austral.

**Highlights:** Tulip fields · Paso Futaleufú · Termas Ventisquero · Puerto Cisnes.

### Day 3 — Puerto Cisnes → Puerto Ingeniero Ibáñez → Chile Chico

20% gravel · 322 km

A scenic road with mountain and fjords, alternating pavement and gravel. The arrival in Puerto Ingeniero Ibáñez marks the end of a scenic stage inside Carretera Austral, while we wait to board the ferry across Lago General Carrera toward Chile Chico.

**Highlights:** Reserva Nacional Mañihuales · Reserva Nacional Lago Las Torres · Parque Nacional Cerro Castillo · Lago General Carrera ferry.

### Day 4 — Chile Chico → Cochrane

70% gravel · 178 km

A pure gravel day with bright views and rare landscapes. We leave Chile Chico riding along Lago General Carrera, one of the largest lakes in Patagonia, with open, constant views of the water for much of the day. Real riding, where the motorcycle and the place set the terms.

**Highlights:** Lago General Carrera · Reserva Nacional Lago General Carrera · Lago Cochrane · Lago Bertrand.

### Day 5 — Cochrane → Paso Roballos → Perito Moreno

80% gravel · 250 km

The most powerful day of the whole trip. Paso Roballos is one of those roads you do not forget. Open steppe, mountains on both sides, and total isolation. Once on the Argentine side, the road follows the cordillera for two hours with views of the Andes. The arrival in Los Antiguos marks the end of the gravel and the return to pavement with Lago General Carrera in sight. The day ends in Perito Moreno.

**Highlights:** Valle Chacabuco · Paso Roballos · Lago Cochrane · Los Antiguos.

### Day 6 — Perito Moreno → Lago Vintter

15% gravel · 473 km

Return day, a long stage on Ruta 40. No technical demand, but many hours in the saddle. The road is isolated, with long paved straights to Gobernador Costa. We leave Ruta 40 and take a secondary road toward Río Pico and Lago Vintter, entering a Patagonia almost nobody knows. Low mountains with the Andes in the background.

**Highlights:** Río Pico.

### Day 7 — Lago Vintter → Bariloche

35% gravel · 467 km

The Lago Vintter to Trevelin section is marked by low forests and snowy mountains. A gravel stretch with no traffic, the road to ourselves. We cross an open, silent Patagonia that does not appear on traditional circuits. After Trevelin, we ride the part of Ruta 40 we did not cover before and the classic section between El Bolsón and Bariloche.

**Highlights:** Lago Vintter · Lago Palena · Ruta 40 classics.
