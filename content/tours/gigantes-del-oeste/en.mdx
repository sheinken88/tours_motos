## What you will ride

Eight days through Mendoza, San Juan, and La Rioja. We cross the Andes through Paso Los Libertadores, sleep at Laguna Brava at 4300 masl, and climb to Mina La Mejicana, one of the most demanding sections in western Argentina. Designed routes. Real timing.

## The route

### Day 1 — Mendoza Capital → Potrerillos → Villavicencio → Uspallata

80% pavement · 218 km

We start the motorcycle tour from Mendoza Capital toward Potrerillos, riding through tunnels, curves, and reservoirs. Then we enter Reserva Natural Villavicencio: gravel, 365 curves, and the historic hotel before sleeping in Uspallata.

**Highlights:** Embalse Potrerillos · Reserva Natural Villavicencio.

### Day 2 — Uspallata → Paso Los Libertadores → Uspallata

90% pavement · 313 km · 3854 masl

A full day crossing the cordillera through the heart of the Andes. Views of Aconcagua, high-mountain tunnels, and the memorable descent into Chile through the famous Caracoles, one of the most iconic sections of the road. We reach Los Andes and return to Uspallata with a stop at Cristo Redentor.

**Highlights:** Puente del Inca · Aconcagua · Cristo Redentor · Cuesta Caracoles.

### Day 3 — Pampa del Leoncito → Laguna Blanca → Calingasta

80% pavement · 290 km

We leave the Mendoza valley and enter open plains and solitary roads. The motorcycle tour continues through Pampa del Leoncito, Barreal, and Laguna Blanca: a day of long horizons and earned sunsets.

**Highlights:** Pampa Leoncito · Laguna Blanca.

### Day 4 — Barreal → Rodeo → Jáchal → Huaco → Villa Unión

100% pavement · 437 km

The most scenic crossing of the tour: valleys, dams, quebradas, and dry La Rioja terrain. Stop in Rodeo. La Ciénaga and Cuesta de Huaco are iconic San Juan places: the road turns into tight curves and opens onto a lookout over the valleys.

**Highlights:** Rodeo · Quebrada de Jáchal · Cuesta de Huaco.

### Day 5 — Villa Unión → Laguna Brava → Volcancito de Troya → Villa Unión

40% pavement · 370 km · 4300 masl

We climb to Laguna Brava by motorcycle, a high-mountain lagoon guarded by silence, flamingos, and stone refuges. The gravel of Quebrada de la Troya and La Herradura adds color and demand to the day.

**Highlights:** Laguna Brava · Volcancito de Troya · Quebrada de la Troya · La Herradura.

### Day 6 — Villa Unión → Talampaya → Valle de la Luna → Chilecito

90% pavement · 386 km

Talampaya gives us red walls and canyons carved by millions of years of geological history. We continue through Valle de la Luna, with eroded shapes and ochre tones. The stage closes with the climb through Cuesta de Miranda, a road of curves and open views, ending in Chilecito, home of the cableway and Mina La Mejicana.

**Highlights:** Talampaya · Valle de la Luna · Cuesta de Miranda.

### Day 7 — Chilecito → Cañón del Ocre → Mina La Mejicana → Chilecito

20% pavement · 240 km · 4600 masl

The climb to Mina La Mejicana by motorcycle is short in kilometers and intense in history, altitude, and landscape. Cañón del Ocre, orange rivers, abandoned structures, and absolute silence close the highest section of the tour.

**Highlights:** Cañón del Ocre · Mina La Mejicana · Los Pesebres.

### Day 8 — Chilecito → Termas de Santa Teresita → La Rioja

100% pavement

The trip closes on Ruta 40 toward Termas de Santa Teresita, with rest before the return to La Rioja.

**Highlights:** Termas de Santa Teresita.
