## What you will ride

Seven days by motorcycle across Salta and Jujuy. Pavement, gravel, altitude, jungle, and Andean villages. Abra del Acay sets the bar: 4895 masl, a route earned kilometer by kilometer.

## The route

### Day 1 — Salta Capital → Cabra Corral → Cuesta del Obispo → Cachi

70% pavement · 270 km · 3300 masl

The first day packs water, jungle, altitude, mountain, and Andean villages into a few kilometers. We leave Salta Capital for Embalse Cabra Corral, where lunch looks over the lake. Then we continue toward Cuesta del Obispo. Curves, cardones, and clouds mark the stage all the way to Cachi, one of the strongest towns Salta gives us.

**Highlights:** Embalse Cabra Corral · Parque Nacional Los Cardones · Cuesta del Obispo · Recta del Tintín.

### Day 2 — Cachi → Quebrada de las Flechas → Cafayate → El Carril

80% pavement · 308 km

A day built to enjoy from the saddle. Playful curves, changes of rhythm, and color across the whole ride. We cross Quebrada de las Flechas, have lunch in Cafayate, and visit its classic wineries. Quebrada de las Conchas, with stops like the Amphitheater, Garganta del Diablo, and El Sapo, turns this section into a day that stays with you.

**Highlights:** Quebrada de las Flechas · Amphitheater · Quebrada de las Conchas · Garganta del Diablo.

### Day 3 — El Carril → Abra del Acay → San Antonio de los Cobres

20% pavement · 4895 masl

Abra del Acay is the most emblematic section of Ruta 40, its highest point and the highest point of any national road in the Americas. The road gives intense colors, open views, and an endless route over the mountain. Cliffs and a vast landscape build with the climb. The descent into San Antonio de los Cobres lowers the difficulty and gives open views with calmer riding.

**Highlights:** Abra del Acay.

### Day 4 — S.A. Cobres → Viaducto La Polvorilla → Tilcara

80% pavement · 293 km · 3890 masl

We ride toward Viaducto La Polvorilla, passing beneath it and through La Juguetería and its huge rock formations. Then we continue to Salinas Grandes, a sharp contrast with the surrounding mountains. On the descent to Purmamarca, tighter curves and colored hills appear before the arrival in town.

**Highlights:** Viaducto La Polvorilla · La Juguetería · Salinas Grandes · Cerro de los 7 Colores.

### Day 5 — Tilcara → Hornocal → Caspala

20% pavement · 140 km · 4550 masl

This day is about the ride and the place more than speed. Tight curves, straight sections beside cliffs, and the feeling of floating above the clouds. It feels like crossing the true north: less traveled, tied hard to the mountain, with a constant run of landscapes that make you stop, look, and keep moving.

**Highlights:** Hornocal · Abra Azul · Caspala.

### Day 6 — Caspala → Parque Nacional Calilegua → Libertador San Martín

50% pavement · 205 km

A day that changes the whole scene. From high roads and isolated villages, we begin to descend into the Yungas jungle through Parque Nacional Calilegua. The route turns green, humid, and closed in, with curves through dense vegetation and a completely different climate. One transition: from dry mountain to living jungle, ending in Libertador San Martín with the feeling of having crossed two worlds in one day.

**Highlights:** Parque Nacional Calilegua.

### Day 7 — Libertador San Martín → Embalse Las Maderas → Salta

80% pavement · 143 km

A calm day to close the tour. Lunch beside Dique La Ciénaga and around two final hours of pavement and curves back to Salta Capital.
