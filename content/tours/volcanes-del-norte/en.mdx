## What you will ride

Seven days through Catamarca: Termas de Fiambalá, Ruta de los Seismiles, Balcón del Pissis, Campo de Piedra Pómez, and Cuesta de Capillitas. A volcanic crossing that combines precordillera, Puna, and yungas jungle. We design routes that do not show up on the map.

## The route

### Day 1 — San Fernando → Fiambalá

100% pavement · 325 km

We cross Ruta del Adobe, which links Tinogasta and Fiambalá. The road runs through small towns until the arrival in Fiambalá. We reach the cabin, rest, and head to the emblematic Termas de Fiambalá in the Andean precordillera, in a dry, silent, fully natural setting.

**Lodging:** Viñedos de la Luna.
**Highlights:** Ruta del Adobe · Termas de Fiambalá.

### Day 2 — Fiambalá → Balcón del Pissis → Fiambalá

70% pavement · 30% gravel · 280 km · 4500 masl

Ruta de los Seismiles is one of the most powerful roads in the region, surrounded by mountains and volcanoes. The road enters Quebrada de las Angosturas, a narrow, winding section between volcanic rock walls. It opens into a wide valley and gains altitude up to Balcón del Pissis, a natural lookout with a direct view of Volcán Pissis. Access also reaches Laguna Verde and Laguna Azul.

**Highlights:** Balcón del Pissis · Quebrada de las Angosturas · Ruta de los Seismiles · Lagunas Verde, Azul, and Negra.

### Day 3 — Fiambalá → Belén

100% pavement · 287 km

We ride north toward the massive Dunas de Tatón, one of the largest and most isolated sand fields in Catamarca. Then we continue toward Tinogasta, with a stop and visit at Veralma winery. We pass the iconic km 4040 of Ruta 40 on the way to Belén.

**Highlights:** Dunas de Tatón · Km 4040 · Belén.

### Day 4 — Belén → Antofagasta de la Sierra

90% pavement · 10% gravel · 259 km · 3500 masl

The route combines mountain, quebradas, and the absolute scale of the Puna. After the climb, the landscape opens: endless straights, total silence. The arrival in Antofagasta gives views of more than four volcanoes and the lava field of Volcán Alumbrera, a rare place in Argentina.

**Lodging:** Hospedaje El Sendero.
**Highlights:** Cuesta de Randolfo · Pucará la Alumbrera · Lava fields.

### Day 5 — Antofagasta de la Sierra → Campo de Piedra Pómez

30% pavement · 70% gravel · 200 km · 3500 masl

The crossing moves through extreme volcanic terrain. The ground turns pale and rolling until we enter Campo de Piedra Pómez, a formation unique in the world. The ride continues toward Laguna Carachi Pampa: a high lagoon surrounded by volcanic plains, where the water reflects the sky and the hills. Short in kilometers, huge in experience.

**Lodging:** Hospedaje El Sendero.
**Highlights:** Campo de Piedra Pómez · Laguna Carachi Pampa · Volcán Carachi Pampa.

### Day 6 — El Peñón → Andalgalá

50% pavement · 50% gravel · 340 km

Leaving El Peñón, the dry volcanic landscape gives way to increasingly scenic mountain roads. A full gravel day through Cuesta de Capillitas, the longest cuesta in South America. We visit Refugio del Minero and finish the day in Andalgalá.

**Highlights:** Cuesta Minas Capillitas · Refugio del Minero.

### Day 7 — Andalgalá → San Fernando

70% pavement · 30% gravel · 226 km

As the road gains altitude, watercourses, dense vegetation, and mountain jungle appear, creating a total contrast with the dry days before. The passage through Parque Nacional Aconquija is one of the day's great moments: yungas forest, constant curves, natural lookouts, and a fresh, living environment. A very scenic section, built to enjoy the riding and the change of climate.

**Highlights:** Cuesta la Chilca · Loma Larga.
