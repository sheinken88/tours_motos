# Design System — Moto On/Off

> Visual baseline: **two-zone pulp poster**. Alternating bands of saturated burgundy-red and warm kraft paper, both heavily textured with print grain and grunge. Halftone cutouts bleed across band boundaries. Display type is wood-block / slab condensed with rough edges. Outlined sticker-frame CTAs. Hand-torn paper divisions between sections.
>
> Heritage references: 1970s rally posters, vintage motorcycle club zines, screen-printed travel propaganda, Hunter S. Thompson book covers, Mexican lotería cards, kraft-paper newsprint.
>
> This document is the source of truth for visual language, tokens, and component patterns. Read it before building or modifying any UI.

---

## 1. Visual Identity

### Aesthetic

**Two-zone pulp poster.** Every page is composed as a series of alternating colored bands — burgundy-red zones for hero/storytelling moments, kraft-paper zones for content/details — torn from each other along irregular paper edges, with halftone illustrations bleeding across the seams. Not a website that _contains_ posters; a stack of posters glued together into a website.

for reference look at: /Users/sheinken/Desktop/tour_motos/inspiration.png

### Emotional tone

- **Triumph, not tourism.** The brand sells personal victory — what the rider _becomes_ by completing the route, not the route itself.
- **Earned, not curated.** Pride in achievement, not comfort delivered. The mud, the sand, the challenge are the _product_.
- **Camaraderie over solitude.** Group silhouettes, shared fires, riders next to riders. We are facilitators of discovery, not soloists.
- **Confident, not defensive.** The brand never frames itself in opposition to luxury competitors — it stands on its own claim. We say what we _are_, not what we aren't.
- **Wood-block, not letterpress.** Display type feels carved and stamped, not finely printed.

### Strategic positioning

**The competition sells tours. We sell triumph.**

Adventure tourism competitors converge on a polished, luxury-focused aesthetic — they make sure no one gets dirty. We position around the rider's personal victory: pride in what they've achieved, the discovery of what they're made of. The mud, the sand, and the challenge aren't obstacles to apologize for — they're the product, the thing the rider conquers.

People don't pay to suffer. They pay to find out what they're made of, and we facilitate that discovery. Instead of showing off luxury, we show personal victories.

Visual differentiation reinforces this positioning: the gonzo/pulp aesthetic is the visual equivalent of triumph-over-terrain — raw, earned, unmistakable. The brand never _says_ it isn't luxury (defensive); it _embodies_ something different (confident).

### Stylistic influences

- **1960s–70s rally and ISDT posters** — bold display type over halftone action shots, warm paper stock.
- **Vintage motorcycle club newsletters and zines** — kraft-paper printing, wood-block headlines, X-bullet lists.
- **Mexican lotería cards and lucha libre posters** — saturated single colors, torn-paper composition, sticker overlap.
- **Gonzo journalism print covers** — Hunter S. Thompson energy, irreverent typography.
- **Screen-printed travel propaganda** — WPA-era national parks posters in their roughest editions.
- **DIY punk flyers** — torn paper, stamped icons, hand-cut compositions, no preciousness.

### Target audience (reaffirmed)

Riders who measure trips by what they accomplished, not where they slept. They want to come home changed — with a story about a pass they cleared, a river they crossed, a day they didn't think they'd survive. Predominantly 30–55, ride ADV bikes (BMW GS, Tenere, KTM, Royal Enfield), value mastery over comfort.

## 1.5 Brand Voice & Messaging

The visual system and the copy must reinforce each other. The brand voice belongs in the design system because tone-deaf copy on a brilliantly designed page kills the whole composition.

### Core positioning

**The competition sells tours. We sell triumph.**

### Voice principles

**1. Sell what the rider becomes, not what we provide.**
Every hero-tier sentence answers _"who does the rider get to be at the end of this trip?"_ — never _"what amenities are included?"_ The product is the transformation, not the route.

**2. Confident, never defensive.**
The brand stands on its own positive claim. It does not define itself in opposition to luxury competitors. It does not say "we're not a 5-star hotel" or "we don't pamper you" — it doesn't need to. Mentioning what we aren't gives the competition rent-free space in our copy.

**3. Verb-first. Active. Past tense for proof, present tense for promise.**

- _Crossed the pass at 4,800m._ (Past — proof.)
- _Ride routes that test what you're made of._ (Present — promise.)
- Avoid: _We offer rides..._, _Our tours include..._, _You will experience..._

**4. The challenge is the product, never the obstacle.**
Mud, sand, altitude, distance, fatigue — name them as the things the rider conquers, not as warnings or apologies. _Two thousand kilometers of gravel, wind, and high desert. That's the route._

**5. First-person plural, founder-led.**
"We ride" beats "the company offers." The voice should always feel like one of the founders is talking to you, not a marketing team. This is the difference between "Discover the magic of Patagonia" and "We've ridden this route eleven times. Here's what it'll cost you."

**6. Earned vocabulary.**
Triumph. Conquer. Earned. Remembered. Ridden. Tested. Crossed. These are signature words. Use them with intention; don't dilute them by spreading them across every paragraph.

### Headline patterns

| Pattern               | Example                                           | Why it works                                                 |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Achievement claim** | _"Trips that leave marks."_                       | What the rider takes away, not what we deliver.              |
| **Identity claim**    | _"For riders who ride to conquer."_               | Defines the audience by what they want, not who they reject. |
| **Place + verb**      | _"Cross the Andes the hard way."_                 | Concrete, action-led, geographic.                            |
| **Proof statement**   | _"Twelve days. 2,200 kilometers. One Patagonia."_ | Numbers and place names earn trust without bragging.         |
| **Question**          | _"What are you made of?"_                         | Used sparingly — once per page max. Direct, personal.        |

### Anti-patterns — DO NOT WRITE

| Avoid                                                 | Why                                                               | Replace with                                                                                                 |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| _"I don't want a 5-star hotel, I want to ride."_      | Defines us by what we aren't. Mentions the competition's product. | _"I want to ride."_ / _"Ride to conquer."_                                                                   |
| _"No pampering. No parade."_                          | Negative list. Defensive.                                         | _"Real routes. Real riders. Real distance."_                                                                 |
| _"Comfort kills the edge."_                           | Implies we know the rider wants discomfort (they don't).          | _"Routes that test what you're made of."_                                                                    |
| _"You'd rather be dirty than impressed."_             | Frames the audience by what they reject.                          | _"You ride to find out what you're made of."_                                                                |
| _"Stories worth telling."_                            | Empty marketing platitude.                                        | _"You'll talk about this trip for years."_ (specific) or just describe the trip and let the reader conclude. |
| _"Real connections. Real stories. Real hospitality."_ | Triple-real is a tell that nothing here is real.                  | Pick one specific moment and describe it.                                                                    |
| _"Discover" / "Adventure awaits" / "Embark"_          | Travel-brochure verbs that mean nothing.                          | Verbs that describe what actually happens — _cross, climb, ride, sleep, eat, wake._                          |

### Concrete > abstract. Always.

_"Mountains. Desert. Epic."_ is a placeholder, not copy. _"4,200m passes, the Sahara at dawn, ten days self-supported"_ is copy. Specifics earn trust; abstractions ask for it. When in doubt, name the place, the distance, the elevation, the day count.

### CTA copy

| Use                                 | Avoid         |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------- |
| `Plan your trip`                    | `Get started` |
| `See the routes` / `View all trips` | `Learn more`  |
| `Read the journal`                  | `Our blog`    |
| `Talk to us` / `Let's ride`         | `Contact us`  |
| `Hold a spot`                       | `Book now`    |

CTAs should feel like an invitation to a specific next step, not a generic transaction.

### The 5-word test

Every hero-tier headline should pass: _"Could a luxury competitor put this exact phrase on their site?"_ If yes, rewrite. If a Belmond-tier travel agency could say _"Adventure awaits"_, we shouldn't say it either. Our copy must be unmistakably ours.

```css
--color-danger: #7a2018;
--color-info: #3d556c;
```

### The two-zone rule

**Red and paper are equal partners.** Pages alternate between red zones (hero, manifesto, calls-to-action, tour grids) and paper zones (feature strips, content blocks, testimonials, footer-adjacent sections). The rhythm of red→paper→red→paper is what distinguishes this design from a single-color flat field.

In red zones:

- Primary text is paper-colored.
- Headlines are paper-colored display type.
- CTAs are outlined paper-colored frames.
- Halftones are ink-colored.

In paper zones:

- Primary text is ink-colored.
- **Headlines are red-colored display type** (this is critical — red headlines on paper are a signature pattern).
- CTAs are outlined ink-colored frames.
- Halftones remain ink-colored.

### Contrast strategy

Three-tone hierarchy at all times:

1. **Field** (red OR paper, ~60% of any zone)
2. **Ink halftone illustrations** (silhouettes, photos, ~30%)
3. **Display highlights** (paper-on-red OR red-on-paper headlines, ~10%)

Any time a fourth color tries to enter (a green icon, a blue link, a gradient), reject it. The system breaks if expanded.

### Texture color principle

Pure flat fills look wrong in this aesthetic. **Every surface gets a paper-grain texture overlay** at meaningful opacity (8–15%). This is non-negotiable — flat red and flat paper both look digital. Plus an additional grunge-and-scuff layer on red zones to suggest aged ink. See §11 for production technique.

### Accessibility

- Paper on red (`#E8DCC4` on `#A8342A`): contrast ratio ~6.8:1 — passes WCAG AA for all sizes, AAA for large text.
- Ink on paper (`#1F140E` on `#E8DCC4`): contrast ratio ~12.5:1 — passes AAA universally.
- **Red on paper** (`#A8342A` on `#E8DCC4`): contrast ratio ~4.6:1 — passes AA for normal text (≥18px regular or ≥14px bold). **Use this primarily for headlines and emphasis, not for body copy.**
- **Never** put body copy in muted-on-red below 16px — drops below AA.
- Focus rings: 2px paper outline with 2px offset on red surfaces; 2px ink outline with 2px offset on paper surfaces.

## 3. Typography System

### Font families

| Role                                                                           | Recommended                                                            | Free alternative                                                             | Why                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Display headlines** (`I DON'T WANT A 5-STAR HOTEL`)                          | **Knockout HTF-93 Ultimate Sumo** or **Tablet Gothic Condensed Heavy** | **Anton** or **Big Shoulders Display 800** with a wood-block distress filter | Heavy condensed slab/sans, must look carved/stamped not printed. Squared terminals, slight imperfection.                       |
| **Sub-display / brand wordmark**                                               | **Trade Gothic Bold Condensed** or **Industry Bold**                   | **Oswald 600–700**                                                           | Used for `RUST & ROAD`-style logos, section labels. Clean condensed sans.                                                      |
| **Body / UI**                                                                  | **Söhne** or **Inter**                                                 | **Inter** (400–600)                                                          | Clean grotesque counterweight to the display chaos. Body must be legible.                                                      |
| **Italic accents** (`MOTORCYCLE EXPEDITIONS FOR RIDERS WHO RIDE TO *CONQUER*`) | **Knockout Italic** or italic of display family                        | **Anton + skew transform** (acceptable workaround)                           | Italic display in supporting copy, used sparingly for emphasis.                                                                |
| **Hand-script accents** (sparse use)                                           | **Permanent Marker** or **Beastly**                                    | **Permanent Marker**                                                         | Used only for hand-written-style annotations like a "RIDDEN. EARNED. REMEMBERED." sticky-note. Maximum 1–2 instances per page. |

> **Implementation:** load Anton (display) and Inter (body) via `next/font`. Hand-script font loads only on routes that need it (lazy import). Three font files maximum across the site.

### Distress treatment

Display type must look **printed and aged, not rendered**. The reference shows **wood-block carving** energy — squared terminals, occasional ink-fill, edge erosion. Two production paths:

1. **SVG with composite filter** (preferred for headlines):

   ```css
   .display-distressed {
     filter: url(#woodblock-distress);
   }
   ```

   The SVG filter combines `feTurbulence` (high baseFrequency for fine grain) + `feDisplacementMap` (subtle, 1–2 scale) + `feComposite` against a transparent grunge texture. Defined once in a global SVG sprite.

2. **Pre-rendered PNG/SVG headlines** for hero-tier display moments where filter performance is a concern. Generated in Photoshop/Affinity with paper texture multiplied over the type using **Subtract** or **Multiply** blend modes at 60–80% opacity.

Body and UI text **never** get distress treatment — illegibility kills conversion.

### Weights & casing

- Display: **800–900**, **always uppercase** for hero/section openers.
- Sub-display brand marks: **700**, uppercase, tight tracking.
- Body manifesto copy: **500**, mixed case, occasionally uppercase for short blocks (3–4 lines max).
- Body running copy: **400**, mixed case, never uppercase past 2 lines.
- UI labels and feature titles: **700**, uppercase, tracked.
- Hand-script: **400**, mixed case, used 1–2 times per page max.

### Line height & tracking

```css
--leading-display-tight: 0.92; /* Hero killers — tight but breathing */
--leading-display: 0.98;
--leading-tight: 1.1;
--leading-normal: 1.5;
--leading-relaxed: 1.65;

--tracking-display: -0.015em; /* Slightly negative for huge condensed type */
--tracking-eyebrow: 0.12em;
--tracking-cta: 0.1em;
--tracking-uppercase: 0.06em;
--tracking-normal: 0;
```

### Underline as decoration

The reference uses **hand-drawn underlines** under emphasis words (`CONQUER`, `EARNED`, divider strokes under section openers). Implement as inline SVG that renders an irregular brush stroke, **not** `text-decoration: underline`.

```html
<span class="emphasized">
  CONQUER
  <svg class="hand-underline" aria-hidden="true">…</svg>
</span>
```

Use sparingly — once per screen, on the single most important word.

## 4. Layout & Spacing

### Composition philosophy

**Stacked posters, not flowing layout.** Each section is a discrete colored band. Section transitions are visible — torn paper edges between zones, halftone illustrations bleeding across the boundary, occasional cutout figures spanning two zones. The 12-column grid still exists underneath as scaffolding, but band boundaries dominate the visual rhythm.

### Grid

```css
--container-max: 1440px;
--container-content: 1240px;
--container-narrow: 720px;
--container-padding: clamp(1.25rem, 4vw, 4rem);
```

Use a 12-column grid with 24px gutters as the _default_, then escape it deliberately:

- Display headlines may extend past content padding into the gutter.
- Cutout silhouettes bleed off the right or bottom edge of a zone.
- Halftone illustrations cross zone boundaries (e.g., bike+rider extending from red hero into paper feature strip below).
- Sticker callouts rotate and overlap zone edges.

### Spacing scale (4px base)

```css
--space-2xs: 4px;
--space-xs: 8px;
--space-sm: 12px;
--space-md: 16px;
--space-lg: 24px;
--space-xl: 32px;
--space-2xl: 48px;
--space-3xl: 64px;
--space-4xl: 96px;
--space-5xl: 128px;
--space-6xl: 160px; /* Hero vertical breathing room */
```

- **Zone vertical padding**: `--space-3xl` to `--space-4xl` (64–96px) top and bottom of each colored band.
- **Hero top padding** (below floating nav): `--space-3xl` minimum on desktop.
- **Card padding**: `--space-xl` to `--space-2xl`.
- **Sticker CTA padding**: `--space-md` vertical, `--space-xl` horizontal.
- **Feature strip icon → label gap**: `--space-sm`.
- **Inter-zone seam**: zero margin. Zones butt directly against each other; the torn paper edge is the visual gap.

### Alignment patterns

- Display headlines: **left-aligned**, ragged right. **Never centered.**
- Manifesto copy blocks: left-aligned, max 4–6 lines, sit beneath the headline.
- Feature strips: equal columns, left-aligned within each column.
- Sticker callouts: tilted (`rotate(-2deg)` to `rotate(3deg)`), placed against an edge or overlapping a halftone illustration.
- Quote blocks and "this is for you if..." lists: left-aligned with X-bullet markers.

### Density

- **Hero zone**: low density. One headline, one manifesto block, one cutout figure. Optional sticky-note callout.
- **Feature strip zone**: medium density. 4 icon+label+caption cells in a row, separated by hairline ink dividers.
- **Tour grid zone**: medium density. 3 cards in a row with halftone photos, ink titles on paper labels, metadata strips.
- **Manifesto / "this is for you" zone**: low-medium density. Headline + bulleted X-list + halftone illustration alongside.
- **Journal zone**: medium density. 3 cards with photo+title+date.

### Responsive behavior

- Display sizing via `clamp()` rather than breakpoint cascades.
- Cutout silhouettes anchor right or bottom on desktop, become full-width banners above/below content on mobile.
- Feature strips: 4-up → 2-up → 1-up.
- Tour cards: 3-up → 2-up → 1-up (carousel optional).
- Mobile nav collapses to hamburger that opens a full-screen red overlay — stays in aesthetic.
- Zone seams (torn paper edges) scale proportionally — never disappear on mobile.

## 5. Components & UI Patterns

### Navigation

**Floating, no background fill.** Nav links sit directly on the red field at the top of the hero. Logo (left, including skull/badge mark), text links (right), primary CTA at far right.

Pattern: gap-separated inline links with a single bordered CTA at the end.

```tsx
<nav className="flex items-center justify-between text-eyebrow uppercase tracking-eyebrow text-paper">
  <Logo /> {/* Skull badge + RUST & ROAD wordmark */}
  <div className="flex items-center gap-xl">
    {links.map((l) => (
      <Link key={l.href} href={l.href}>
        {l.label}
      </Link>
    ))}
    <Button variant="sticker-outline-paper">Let's Ride</Button>
  </div>
</nav>
```

Hover: subtle paper-color underline animates from left, 250ms.

### Hero zone

Composition layers (back to front):

1. **Red field** with paper-grain + grunge texture overlay.
2. **Halftone landscape silhouette** anchored to bottom edge (mountain ridge bleeding into next zone).
3. **Cutout figure** (rider on bike, three-quarter angle) anchored bottom-right, large, bleeding past right margin.
4. **Display headline** top-left, oversized, breaks past the standard content margin.
5. **Manifesto copy block** below headline, paper-color, occasionally with italic emphasis word + hand-underline.
6. **Sticky-note callout** (rotated paper rectangle with hand-script copy) overlapping the cutout figure or anchored to edge.

Why this works: every layer reinforces "this is a printed poster." The torn paper edge at the bottom of the zone signals the transition to the next band.

### Sticker CTA — outlined frame (PRIMARY pattern in this system)

**This is the dominant CTA style.** Visual structure:

- **Rectangle outline** in current color (paper-on-red zones, ink-on-paper zones), 2px stroke.
- **Slightly irregular edges** — not perfect rectangles, with hand-drawn or distress-masked borders.
- **Uppercase tracked text**, display-weight typography.
- **Trailing arrow icon** (`→`).
- **Slight rotation** (`-1deg` to `2deg`).
- **Optional hard-offset shadow** in deep tone.

```css
.sticker-cta-outline {
  background: transparent;
  color: currentColor;
  border: 2px solid currentColor;
  padding: 14px 28px;
  font-family: var(--font-display);
  font-weight: 700;
  text-transform: uppercase;
  letter-spacing: var(--tracking-cta);
  font-size: 0.875rem;
  transform: rotate(-1deg);
  position: relative;
  /* Hand-drawn edge via SVG mask overlay */
}
.sticker-cta-outline::before {
  /* SVG hand-stroke border overlay for distressed look */
}
.sticker-cta-outline:hover {
  transform: rotate(-0.5deg) translateY(-2px);
  background: currentColor;
  color: var(--bg-color); /* inverts on hover */
}
```

In red zones: paper-color outline + paper-color text → fills with paper, text becomes red on hover.
In paper zones: ink-color outline + ink-color text → fills with ink, text becomes paper on hover.

### Sticker CTA — filled (secondary, used sparingly)

Filled paper chip with ink text, slight tilt, hard-offset shadow. Used for the most important conversion CTAs only — primary booking CTAs, newsletter submit, etc. Maximum **one** filled sticker per zone.

### Sticky-note callout

The hand-script note pattern (`RIDDEN. EARNED. REMEMBERED.` or similar — short, declarative, victory-framed).

- **Paper-colored rectangle**, slight rotation (3–5deg).
- **Hand-script font**, ink color.
- **Hand-drawn X or arrow scribble** as decorative element.
- Overlaps the edge of a cutout illustration or zone boundary.

```tsx
<div className="sticky-note absolute -rotate-3 right-12 top-1/2 bg-paper p-md shadow-sticker max-w-[200px]">
  <p className="font-script text-ink text-display-md leading-tight">
    Ridden.
    <br />
    Earned.
    <br />
    Remembered.
  </p>
  <HandDrawnX />
</div>
```

Use **once per page maximum** — overuse cheapens it. Copy should always be **declarative and short** (3–5 words), framed around achievement.

### Tour card

- Halftone-treated photo at the top with paper-colored frame border.
- **Paper-colored title bar** with ink display-weight text.
- **Ink-colored metadata strip** below: region, duration, kilometers, descriptive tagline.
- Card sits on the red field with subtle paper-edge effect (suggests a poster pinned to the wall).
- No CTA inside the card itself — the entire card is clickable.

Hover: card lifts 4px, photo halftone density intensifies slightly, paper-edge shadow grows.

### Feature strip — bordered grid

The 4-cell pattern (`REMOTE ROADS / LOCAL PEOPLE / RIDE HARD / SLEEP SIMPLE`). Each cell:

- **Hairline ink-colored vertical divider** between cells (not all four sides — just verticals).
- **Stamped line icon** at top, ink-colored, ~48px.
- **Bold uppercase label** in ink color.
- **Two- to four-line caption** in lower-weight body type, ink color.
- Cells sit on the **paper zone** with full paper-grain texture beneath.

Icons must look hand-stamped, not vector-pristine. Apply slight grain/distress overlay to all icons. Library options: build a custom set, or treat Lucide/Heroicons with a global SVG distress filter.

### "This is for you if..." X-list

Red zone, paper-color text, **X-icon bullets** (not dots, not checkmarks):

```tsx
<ul className="space-y-md">
  {points.map((point) => (
    <li key={point} className="flex items-start gap-md text-paper">
      <XIcon className="w-5 h-5 mt-1 shrink-0" aria-hidden />
      <span>{point}</span>
    </li>
  ))}
</ul>
```

Pair with a halftone group illustration on the right (riders sitting on a ridge, etc.).

### Quote block

Used for testimonials and founder voice:

- **Display-weight slab/sans** quote, currentColor, sentence case or uppercase.
- **Attribution dash + name** on its own line, smaller, same family.
- Optional **manifesto reinforcement** in italic display below.

Treat quotes as first-class composition elements. Each quote gets its own zone (red OR paper) with breathing room.

### Cutout figure / silhouette

The defining illustrative element. Process:

1. Source a high-contrast photograph (rider, bike, group, landscape).
2. Convert to **two-tone halftone** (ink + transparent) — see §11 production pipeline.
3. Export as PNG with transparency.
4. Place on the field with no border, no shadow, no container.

The figure should feel **cut out with scissors**, not Photoshop-feathered. Edges should be sharp, occasionally rough.

### Halftone landscape banner / horizon strip

Used as section transitions and hero base. Mountains, valleys, road horizons rendered in ink halftone, full-width, anchored to the bottom of a zone. Often **bleeds across** the zone boundary into the next band — a mountain ridge starting in the red hero zone and extending into the paper feature strip below.

### Torn paper edge between zones

The signature transition pattern. When a red zone meets a paper zone (or vice-versa), the boundary is **not a straight horizontal line** — it's a **hand-torn paper edge** rendered as an SVG path.

```tsx
<section className="bg-brand-red">
  {/* zone content */}
  <TornEdgeBottom direction="paper" />  {/* SVG path showing torn edge */}
</section>
<section className="bg-paper">
  {/* zone content */}
</section>
```

Generate **3–4 torn-edge SVG variants** to randomize across pages so the edge doesn't repeat identically. Each edge is ~20–40px tall.

### Stamps & metadata

Stamps and tire-track patterns reinforce the printed-object feel. Date stamps on journal cards, "EXPEDITION ##" stamps on tour pages, tire-track ornaments in footer corners.

```css
.stamp {
  font-family: var(--font-display);
  font-weight: 700;
  text-transform: uppercase;
  border: 2px solid currentColor;
  padding: 6px 12px;
  transform: rotate(-3deg);
  letter-spacing: var(--tracking-cta);
  font-size: 0.75rem;
}
```

### Skull/badge brand mark

Recurring identity element. Used in:

- Logo lockup (skull + wordmark).
- Footer center as a section ornament.
- Stamp/seal moments throughout the site.

Implement as a single SVG sprite component reused at multiple sizes.

### Forms

Forms are the one place the aesthetic relaxes — legibility wins.

- Inputs sit on a **paper-colored panel** with ink text and ink hairline borders.
- The panel itself has torn-edge treatment matching the zone style.
- Submit button is a **filled sticker CTA**.
- Newsletter inline form: paper input + ink arrow button (right side).

### Shadows & depth

- **No standard CSS box-shadows** on UI elements.
- Sticker CTAs (when filled) get a **hard-offset deep-toned shadow** (`4px 4px 0 var(--color-brand-red-deep)` in red zones; `4px 4px 0 var(--color-ink)` in paper zones).
- Cards get a **paper-edge effect** (a 1–2px lighter strip on top/left, suggesting a sheet of paper sitting slightly raised).
- Layering between halftone elements creates apparent depth without any shadow.

### Borders

Borders are common in this system, unlike the previous version:

- **Sticker CTA outlines** (2px, current color).
- **Feature strip vertical dividers** (1px, ink/paper).
- **Hairline rules** under section eyebrows.
- **Stamp borders** (2px, rotated).
- All borders should have slight distress treatment via SVG overlay or pixel-level imperfection — never machine-perfect 2px CSS borders for primary visual borders.

Never `border-radius: anything > 0` on primary UI.

## 6. Motion & Interaction

### Philosophy

**The poster comes alive carefully.** Motion exists to acknowledge interaction, not to entertain. A printed poster doesn't animate; this site is a stack of posters that occasionally winks.

### Hover behaviors

- **Outlined sticker CTAs**: tilt rotates slightly (-1deg → -0.5deg), lifts 2px, **fills with current color** (text inverts to background color). 200ms `ease-out`.
- **Filled sticker CTAs**: tilt + lift + shadow grows. 200ms `ease-out`.
- **Nav links**: paper-color underline grows from left, 250ms `ease-out`.
- **Tour cards**: card lifts 4px, photo halftone density intensifies, paper-edge shadow grows.
- **Cutout figures**: optional 1–2deg rotation on hover for clickable figures (e.g., portrait linking to About).
- **Stars/stamps/skull**: brief rotation jitter on hover (`±2deg`, 150ms).

### Load animations

Hero sequence (one-shot, on initial page load):

1. **Red field** present immediately (no fade — feels broken).
2. **Halftone landscape + cutout figure** fade up from bottom together, 600ms.
3. **Display headline** reveals letter-by-letter via mask, 800ms staggered. (Heaviest motion moment.)
4. **Manifesto block** fades in, 400ms.
5. **Sticky-note callout** drops in with a slight bounce + rotation, 500ms.
6. **Torn paper edge** is present from the start — no animation.

Total hero choreography: ~1.8s. Skip animations if `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` is set — render final state immediately.

### Scroll behavior

- Section headlines fade-in once on enter via `IntersectionObserver`. No re-trigger.
- Halftone landscape banners may have **very subtle** parallax (max 8% offset) to simulate paper layers shifting. Test carefully.
- **No scroll-jacking, no horizontal scrolling, no scaling on scroll.**
- Zone transitions never animate on scroll — torn edges are static visual elements.

### Microinteractions

- **WhatsApp / "Let's Ride" FAB** stamps into view when user scrolls past the hero, with a slight rotation and bounce.
- Form labels: animate from inside the field to above on focus.
- Loading states: a halftone-dot spinner instead of the standard CSS spinner.
- Page transitions: optional 200ms paper-tear or fold effect via Framer Motion's `AnimatePresence`.

### Timing tokens

```css

  1.56,

); /* Slight overshoot for stickers/stamps */
```

## 7. Photography & Art Direction

### Source photography

**Two source pools, processed identically.**

**Pool A — riders, bikes, groups, action.** High-contrast subjects, ideally backlit or in strong directional light. Phone shots are acceptable here — halftone treatment is forgiving. Prefer authenticity (a real client at a real campsite) over staged.

**Pool B — landscapes, mountains, roads, horizons.** Used for halftone banners and section transitions. High dynamic range, dramatic horizon lines.

### Halftone processing pipeline

The most important production specification in the system.

**Recommended approach: pre-process all photos.** Runtime CSS halftone filters are unreliable and performance-expensive at hero sizes.

**Process per image:**

1. Open source photo in **Photoshop** (or **Affinity Photo** / **GIMP**).
2. Convert to grayscale, increase contrast aggressively. Goal: clear separation between subject and background.
3. Apply **Halftone Pattern** filter (Filter → Sketch → Halftone Pattern in Photoshop). Settings:
   - Size: 4–6 (smaller = finer dots, larger = punchier poster feel).
   - Contrast: 30–40.
   - Pattern Type: Dot.
4. Convert white to transparent (Select Color Range on white, delete).
5. Optionally tint remaining ink to `--color-ink` (`#1F140E`) rather than pure black.
6. Export as **PNG with transparency** at 2x final display size for retina.

**Alternative:** use a Photoshop action or Affinity macro to batch-process. Document the action in `/docs/halftone-pipeline.md` so the client can run it on new photos.

**Naming convention:** `{subject}-{location}-halftone.png`, e.g. `rider-patagonia-halftone.png`.

### Cutout treatment

For figures placed on the red field:

1. After halftone processing, use **background removal** (Photoshop Select Subject, or remove.bg API).
2. Refine edges to feel **scissor-cut**, not feathered. Sharp edges, occasional rough zones.
3. Drop shadow is **never** added — figures sit directly on the field.
4. **Paper-colored thin outline** is sometimes added to cutouts in red zones — see hero figure with paper-edge halo. This is optional, not required.

### Art direction for new shoots

If the client commissions new photography:

- Backlight or strong sidelight — flat midday light kills halftone conversion.
- Subjects against sky or distant backgrounds (easier silhouette extraction).
- Wide environmental shots for landscape pool.
- Group shots are gold — three or more riders in frame is a hero asset.
- Shoot dirty: dusty bikes, mud, gear stains. Pristine kills the brand.

### What NOT to use

- Stock photography (kills authenticity instantly).
- Drone footage with cinematic color grading (wrong tone).
- Studio shots of bikes (we're not selling motorcycles).
- Sunset/sunrise heavily-saturated landscape shots that fight the brand red.
- Anything with five-star hotel framing.

## 9. Implementation Priority

When building from scratch, ship in this order:

1. **Tokens + theme** — `tailwind.config.ts`, fonts via `next/font`, paper-grain + red-grunge textures, distress SVG sprite.
2. **Texture pipeline assets** — paper-grain PNG, red-grunge PNG, torn-edge SVGs (4 variants), sticker-edge SVGs (3 variants), halftone Photoshop action documented.
3. **Primitives** — `Button` (sticker-outline + sticker-filled variants), `Eyebrow`, `DisplayHeading`, `Stamp`, `StickyNote`, `XIcon`, `HandUnderline`, `SkullBadge`, `Container`.
4. **Surfaces** — `RedZone`, `PaperZone`, `TornEdge`, `HalftoneImage`, `CutoutFigure`, `LandscapeBanner`, `PaperPanel`.
5. **Layout shell** — `Nav`, `Footer`, `WhatsAppFAB`.
6. **Hero zone** — full poster composition with all six layers including torn edge transition. **Validation gate.**
7. **FeatureStripGrid** — 4-cell bordered icon grid in paper zone.
8. **TourGrid** + tour card design.
9. **XListSection** — "this is for you if..." pattern.
10. **JournalGrid** — journal/blog post grid.
11. **QuoteSection** — first-class quote blocks.
12. **Manifesto** — long-form content block.
13. **Forms** — newsletter inline form, inquiry form.
14. **Motion pass** — load choreography, hover states, FAB stamp-in.
15. **Photography processing** — run halftone pipeline on initial library.
16. **Performance + accessibility audit**.

The hero (step 6) is the validation gate. If the red zone doesn't feel like a printed poster — torn edge included — the rest of the system won't carry it. Iterate until it does.

## 10. Risks & Mistakes to Avoid

### Top risks

| Risk                                                                             | Mitigation                                                                                                                |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Aesthetic becomes costume — looks like a website pretending to be vintage        | Commit hard. No half-measures. If a component breaks the aesthetic, redesign it, don't compromise it.                     |
| Performance death from unoptimized halftone PNGs and texture overlays            | Pre-process all images. Use `next/image` with proper `sizes`. Hero halftone PNGs ≤ 200KB. Texture PNGs tileable + ≤ 50KB. |
| Distress filter destroys legibility                                              | Distress display only. Body and UI stay clean. Test at small sizes.                                                       |
| Single-zone monotony — every page is just red top to bottom                      | Enforce zone alternation: every page has at least 2 red zones AND 2 paper zones.                                          |
| Torn edges become repetitive (same edge on every transition)                     | Randomize among 4 SVG variants. Each page should have visual variety.                                                     |
| Stickers everywhere — every CTA tilted, every card torn                          | Reserve sticker treatment for primary CTAs only. Maximum 2–3 stickers per zone, max 1 filled-sticker per zone.            |
| Hand-script font for body copy or repeated decoration                            | Hand-script max 1–2 instances per page. Sticky-note callout only.                                                         |
| Mobile experience flattens to a generic page                                     | Mobile must keep cutouts, halftone landscapes, sticker CTAs, AND zone alternation. Reduce scale, never aesthetic.         |
| Texture overlays at wrong opacity (too subtle = digital flat; too heavy = noisy) | Paper grain 8–12% opacity. Red grunge 10–15% opacity. Test on 4K display before committing.                               |

### Common mistakes

| Mistake                                                 | Fix                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Using a brighter, cleaner red (Coca-Cola red)           | Stick to burgundy-rust (#A8342A). Brighter reds read as commercial.              |
| Pure black silhouettes (#000000)                        | Use `--color-ink` (#1F140E). Pure black looks digital; warm-black looks printed. |
| Cream/white paper (#FFFFFF or off-white)                | Use kraft (#E8DCC4). Pure white kills the printed feel.                          |
| Smooth photo edges on cutouts                           | Edges should be scissor-sharp. Re-do the cut if it looks feathered.              |
| Adding green/blue functional accents to marketing pages | Reserve functional colors for booking flow only.                                 |
| Centering display type to "balance" the composition     | Always left-aligned. The asymmetry is the brand.                                 |
| Rounding sticker corners                                | Stickers are rectangular with hand-drawn distressed borders.                     |
| Using a real `<u>` underline tag for emphasis           | Use the SVG hand-underline component.                                            |
| Animating everything because Framer Motion is installed | Motion budget: hero choreography on load, hover states, FAB stamp-in. That's it. |
| Putting muted-on-red small text below 16px              | Drops below WCAG AA. Use full-paper opacity at small sizes.                      |
| Straight-line zone transitions                          | Always torn edge. Always SVG. Always one of the variants.                        |
| Missing texture overlay on a zone                       | All zones have texture. No exceptions.                                           |

## 11. Production Pipeline Reference

Quick-reference checklist for new asset creation. Full details in `/docs/halftone-pipeline.md` (to be created).

### New photo → site-ready halftone cutout

1. Source photo (RAW or high-res JPG).
2. Open in Photoshop, increase contrast.
3. Apply Halftone Pattern filter (size 4–6, dot).
4. Tint dots to `--color-ink`.
5. Background removal — clean scissor edges.
6. Export PNG with transparency at 2x display size.
7. Compress with TinyPNG or ImageOptim.
8. Place in `/public/images/halftone/`.
9. Reference via `<HalftoneImage>` component.

### New section → red or paper zone

1. Use `<RedZone>` or `<PaperZone>` wrapper component.
2. Default applies texture overlay automatically (red-grunge or paper-grain).
3. Override texture density via `density="heavy" | "default" | "light"` prop.
4. Add `<TornEdge variant={1-4} direction="up|down" />` between zones at transition points.

### New CTA → outlined sticker

1. Use `<Button variant="sticker-outline">` (default in this system).
2. Color inherits from zone — paper outline in red zones, ink outline in paper zones.
3. Choose sticker-edge variant via `edge={1 | 2 | 3}` prop.
4. Optionally adjust tilt via `tilt="left" | "right" | "none"`.
5. Use `variant="sticker-filled"` for top-priority CTAs only — max 1 per zone.
